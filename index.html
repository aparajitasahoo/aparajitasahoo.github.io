About Me



I am a Data Scientist at IBM Global Business Services. During my tenure at IBM, I have worked on projects related to chatbots, financial modeling and market segmentation for industries spanning across pharmaceutical, banking and health insurance.



I joined IBM after graduating from Carnegie Mellon University with a Masters in Information Systems Management. In my graduate school, my focus was on data science and technology management. As a research assistant, I have worked on projects in the areas of longitudinal patient monitoring and studying the impact of social determinants of health on general population.



In India, I worked on my passion for creating a social impact by working at the intersection of health technologies, data science, design and social innovation with the potential to impact the lives of people globally at the REDX Innovation Lab (now known as REDX WeSchool) in Mumbai. Being one of the founding members with a group of brilliant engineers and designers, I have the experience of starting a Healthcare Innovation Lab in India, driven by the joint vision of researchers from Camera Culture Group (MIT), WeSchool and TATA Trusts.



At REDX, I have worked on researching and developing several low cost healthcare prototypes. We improved their user experience by combining the power of data science, mobile applications and portable hardware design.



This was also a great opportunity for me to gain the entrepreneurial experience of forming partnerships with local hospitals, product suppliers, makerspaces, healthcare professionals and build an active community around the lab. I have gone through the full lifecycle of product development which comprised of brain storming and design thinking for problem identification, user-centered designing and prototyping, testing, getting user feedback, iterating, validating, publishing papers and presenting in public platforms, securing intellectual property rights and launching the product.
