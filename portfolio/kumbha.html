Kumbha Mela

Health Camp in Nashik

In August 2015, I assisted doctors in conducting a free Health Check up camp in Nashik Kumbha mela which is an annual gathering of millions of devotees in India. I, along with my other colleagues from the REDX Innovation Lab, assisted several doctors in Nashik in conducting these free health check ups. The health camp saw a foot fall of hundreds of sadhus and civilians during the period of one week. I interacted with over 500 sadhus and assisted doctors in gathering vital information related to the health conditions of these devotees. I got to personally experience the demographic health diversity present across India and understand how the health information collected from patients varies from one region to another.



When I interacted with the sadhus (saints) who came to the health camp.





I learnt a lot while interacting with people from all over India and understood the complexity and variations in health data in people living in different demographic regions. I also realised the need and importance of primary health screening for people belonging to rural areas who do not have access to proper medical facilities.
